HOW IT THINKS
AI Doesn’t Think — It Predicts
Input: "The capital of India is ___" AI sees: ["The", "capital", "of", "India", "is"] AI predicts next token: "New" (92% confidence) Then predicts: "Delhi" (97% confidence) Output: "The capital of India is New Delhi"
🧩
Tokens
AI breaks text into small pieces called tokens. "Hello world" = 2 tokens.
🎲
Probability
For each token, AI calculates which word is most likely to come next.
🔍
Context
AI uses ALL previous words to decide the next one. More context = better answers.
This is why better prompts = better answers. More context helps AI predict what you actually want.

💡 Key Insight
AI picks the highest probability word, then repeats — one word at a time. That’s why it’s called a language model.
⚠️ This is why AI “hallucinates”
If no word has high confidence, AI still picks one — AI is biased toward giving an answer even when uncertain — it rarely says “I don’t know” on its own.

Agentic AI
AI that DOES — sends emails, browses the web, takes actions
🎤 Karthik Ravishankar
THE KEY DIFFERENCE
Generative AI vs Agentic AI

📝 AI that CREATES

  • Prompt: “Write me an email to my professor asking for a deadline extension”
  • AI generates the text
  • YOU copy-paste and send it manually

⚡ AI that DOES

  • Prompt: “Send an email to my professor asking for a deadline extension”
  • AI opens browser, navigates to email
  • AI types the email and clicks send
  • AI did everything
Same task. Completely different behavior.
🎓 Imagine telling AI to fill your exam registration form or auto-submit an assignment to your college portal

🎭 What is Playwright?
A tool that controls a web browser programmatically — click buttons, fill forms, navigate pages. Normally used by developers for testing websites.
🔌 How MCP Connects Them

Swap Playwright for another MCP server and AI can control databases, calendars, Slack, GitHub, smart devices — anything with an MCP connector.
03
RAG — Retrieval-Augmented Generation
Teaching AI to look things up before answering
CORE CONCEPT
What is RAG?
Retrieval Augmented Generation
⚠️ The Problem
AI models are trained on general internet data. They don’t know about:
  • Your college syllabus
  • Your company’s internal docs
  • Anything after their training date
So they guess — and often get it wrong.
✅ The Solution: RAG
Give AI your documents first, then ask questions. Now it:
  • Retrieves relevant text from your file
  • Augments the prompt with that context
  • Generates an answer based on real data
Answers come from your source, not imagination.
💡 Think of it Like an Open-Book Exam
Without RAG = Closed-book exam
AI relies on memory alone — might be wrong
With RAG = Open-book exam
AI looks up the textbook — answers are accurate
Tools that use RAG:  NotebookLM ChatGPT + Files Perplexity Claude + Docs

⚠️ Without YOUR Documents

  • Same question: “What are the topics in Chapter 2?”
  • Answer: Generic/hallucinated topics that SOUND right but aren’t
  • No sources — made up from training data
  • Risky — could lead you to study the wrong material
RAG = Retrieval Augmented Generation
Give AI your documents → it retrieves relevant info → generates accurate answers. Always give AI your context.

STAY SAFE
AI Safety & Deepfakes

⚠️ The Risks

  • Voice cloning — A 3-second clip is enough to clone your voice
  • Face swap videos — Realistic deepfake videos created in minutes
  • AI-generated scam messages — Phishing emails that sound exactly like your boss
🎬 Deepfake Demo Video

🛡️ Protect Yourself

  • Verify through a second channel — Call the person directly
  • Don’t trust urgency — Scammers pressure you to act fast
  • Look for subtle signs — Odd blinking, warped edges, robotic tone
